Common Router IP Addresses

Find the default IP address for your router's admin panel. Most routers use one of these common IP addresses.

How to Find Your Router's IP Address

  • 1

    Check Your Router

    Look for a sticker on the bottom or back of your router. Many manufacturers print the default IP address directly on the device.

  • 2

    Windows: Command Prompt

    Open Command Prompt and type ipconfig, then look for "Default Gateway". This is your router's IP address.

  • 3

    Mac: System Preferences

    Go to System Preferences > Network > Advanced > TCP/IP and look for "Router". This is your router's IP address.

  • 4

    Android: Settings

    Go to Settings > Wi-Fi > Tap your network > Advanced > IP settings and look for "Gateway". This is your router's IP address.

  • 5

    iOS: Settings

    Go to Settings > Wi-Fi > Tap (i) next to your network and look for "Router". This is your router's IP address.

Troubleshooting IP Address Issues

  • Can't access the admin panel? Make sure you're connected to your router's network via Wi-Fi or Ethernet cable.
  • Address not working? Try the alternative IP addresses listed above for your router brand.
  • Browser timeout? Clear your browser cache or try a different browser. Some routers work better with specific browsers.
  • Still having trouble? Try a factory reset by pressing and holding the reset button on your router for 10-30 seconds (note: this will erase all custom settings).
  • Alternative access methods: Some routers offer domain names like "routerlogin.net" or "router.asus.com" that redirect to your router's interface.
